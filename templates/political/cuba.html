In 1959, Fidel Castro became the leader of Cuba after overthrowing Fulgencio Batista—causing distrust between the USA and Cuba due to Castro being a Communist. This lead to a failed attempt to overthrow Fidel Castro, called the Bay of Pigs, on April 17, 1961. The idea to train Cuban exiles to overthrow Fidel Castro was originally Dwight Eisenhower’s idea; but since Eisenhower left office, JFK was the one blamed for this “botched invasion.”

“In October 1962, an American U-2 spy plane secretly photographed nuclear missile sites being built by the Soviet Union on the island of Cuba.”

“Cuban Missile Crisis”, John F. Kennedy Presidential Library and Museum

As a result of this, President Kennedy placed a blockade around Cuba to stop the importation of more Soviet weapons. This event, called the Cuban Missile Crisis, caused people around the world to be in constant terror for several months until JFK demanded for Khrushchev, the leader of the Soviet Union at that time, to remove all signs of nuclear weaponry on Cuba. The Soviet Union and the United States signed a deal to remove all of the Soviet weapon sites on Cuba in return for the USA not invading Cuba. In a separate deal, the Soviet Union secretly asked the US to remove their nuclear armaments from Turkey as well.
